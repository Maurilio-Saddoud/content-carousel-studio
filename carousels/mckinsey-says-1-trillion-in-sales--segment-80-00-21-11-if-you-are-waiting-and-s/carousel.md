# The internet was built to block bots.

Now that same infrastructure is blocking a new kind of customer.

---

# For 20 years, companies treated bots like spam.

So we built logins,
CAPTCHAs,
rate limits,
and friction everywhere.

That made sense in the old world.

---

# But if AI agents start acting on behalf of real buyers,
those old defenses become conversion killers.

The fences stay the same.
The customer changes.

---

# That is the strategic shift most teams are underestimating.

They are still asking,
"How do we keep bots out?"

Soon the smarter question becomes,
"How do we let trusted agents complete the transaction?"

---

# The companies that adapt first will feel easier to buy from.

Everyone else will look broken,
slow,
and hostile to the next interface layer.
